Itinerary

Below, you'll find a general itinerary for the day of the ceremony. Upon your arrival, we will provide guests with a more detailed schedule.

  • 1:30 pm - Leave campsite for ceremony at Sunset Point Overlook*
  • 2:30 pm - Ceremony begins
  • 2:45 pm - Photos with family and friends
  • 3:15 pm - Guests return to the campsite
  • 5:00 pm - Reception, LET'S PARTY!
  • 10:00 pm - Campsite quite hours begin

Additonal Notes

  • Checkin begins at 3:00 pm, Checkout is at 10:00 am
  • Gravel paths around the campsite (appropriate footwear recommended)
  • Camping chairs provided
  • First aid kits and backup items available
  • Water and snacks accessible 24/7 (all food must be kept in vehicles)
  • Fire pits available both nights
  • Onsite dining available. Coffee, tea, and grab-and-go snacks for purchase. We will also provide some snacks for guests.
  • Bryce Canyon has authorized our party to arrive in a maximum of 10 vehicles. Please do your best to make new homies and carpool with at least 4 people per vehicle.
  • All park entrance fees will be covered. Sean will collect license plate and driver info for each vehicle.

Planning

Packing List

    Attire:

  • Semi-formal attire for the ceremony and reception
  • Comfortable walking shoes (for canyon and campsite)
  • Layers for fluctuating temperatures (warm days, cool mornings/evenings)
  • Warm clothing for chilly nights (elevation: 7,600 ft)
  • Hiking attire and daypacks (for those interested)
  • Fire pit attire (clothing may smell of smoke)

    • Miscellaneous:

  • Flashlights and/or headlamps (some extras available)
  • Umbrellas or raincoats (ponchos provided)
  • Camelback or water bottle (Nalgene bottles provided)
  • Sunscreen and bug repellent (extras available)
  • USB battery packs (some provided)
  • USB and 12-volt charging capabilities (Kacy's van)
  • Camera equipment for those interested in stargazing photography

Weather

The historical average temperature for the last week in August is a high of 76°F to a low of 41°F. Be prepared for warm sunny days and chilly mornings and evenings. Wood burning stoves are provided in each tent.
